About Me

Current

I spend most days working on projects to hone and enhance the skills in my toolbox. I'm working on documenting my journey and sharing my knowledge through my blog (coming soon), and am actively accepting freelance clients as I prepare to navigate the tech world for my first official role as a web developer. I, like this portfolio, will always be a work in progress. Don't hesitate to get in touch!

Beginnings

I entered the workforce at the age of 14 as a volunteer receptionist for an exotic avian boarding facility. Soon after that I was hired at the same facility and so began my long lasting journey as some mix between receptionist, administrative assistant, manager, etc. I was good at this sort of work, so I kept going deeper.

Breaking Point

Later in life I realized I was no longer engaged in my work. It was difficult to face the music, to realize that being good at something didn't mean I enjoyed it, or wanted to continue on this path. I had hit my peak and began searching for my passion.

Discovery

I began by reflecting on what I did and did not enjoy in past positions. I then thought about what I wanted out of life and where my career fit in to those plans. Finally I assessed my skills to determine which I was most interested in, which needed some work, and which I did not want to pursue.

Intro to Tech

In a few of my mixed-administrative roles I was given the opportunity to work closely with various tech professionals. First, as a manager I oversaw the update of the company's website and worked directly with the developer on SEO and design for the new site. Then as an office administrator I was thrown in to the deep-end of wordpress and asked to figure it out. Finally, as an administrative assistant I was tasked with working along side a DBA to update the departments Microsoft Access Database features and functionality.

Revelation

These experiences stood out positively in my reflections. I realized I was tired of supporting these roles, I instead wanted to be in one. Thus began my newly found passion and desire to break in to the wonderful world of computer science/tech/web development.
